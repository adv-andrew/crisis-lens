# CrisisLens Video Presentation Guide

## Target Length: 2:00 (hard limit)

---

## Structure

### 1. THE SKIT + HOOK (0:00 – 0:20)

**PERSON 1** sits at a desk, papers in hand, news anchor voice:

> "Tonight's top stories: the conflict in Ukraine continues. The humanitarian situation in Gaza remains dire. World leaders—"

*Pauses. Shuffles papers. Looks confused.*

> "South Sudan? 9.9 million people? We don't... cover that?"

**Cut to PERSON 2**, casual:

> "That's the problem. Funding follows headlines, not need. So we built CrisisLens."

---

### 2. DEMO (0:20 – 1:20)

Screen recording, Person 2 narrates. Move fast, no dead air.

**PERSON 2 (voiceover):**

*(Landing page — 5 sec)*
> "CrisisLens computes an Overlooked Crisis Index — one score that combines severity, funding gaps, population impact, and media attention."

*(Map page — 10 sec)*
> "The map shows every crisis scored and ranked. Darker red, more overlooked. South Sudan, Syria, Yemen — top three."

*(Crisis Drilldown — 10 sec)*
> "Drill into any country. South Sudan: Camp Coordination, 100% funding gap. Logistics, 99%. Almost nothing has arrived."

*(Reallocation Simulator — 15 sec)*
> "What if we redistributed just 15% of funding from well-covered crises to the most overlooked?"
*(drag slider)*
> "That's the impact. One policy change."

*(Forecast — 10 sec)*
> "And the gap is widening. Statistically significant. This is an early warning system."

*(Recommender — 10 sec)*
> "We also benchmark 8,000 projects. The top performers achieve 15.6x the median efficiency. Our recommender finds the best models to scale."

---

### 3. TECH + PAPER (1:20 – 1:40)

**Cut to PERSON 3**, on camera with the formula on screen behind them:

> "The OCI multiplies four signals: people in need over population, severity, funding gap, and a media neglect multiplier from Google Trends. Four signals, one number."

> "The full pipeline runs on Databricks with Delta Lake. And we wrote a research paper documenting every finding."

*(Flash paper PDF for 1 second)*

---

### 4. THE CLOSE (1:40 – 2:00)

**Cut to PERSON 4** (or all 4 together):

> "91.7% average funding gap. 13 crises with almost zero media attention. Millions of people, and nobody's watching."

> "The crises that don't make headlines still need funding. CrisisLens makes sure they're not invisible."

**End card:** Team name, GitHub link. 5 seconds.

## Speaker Assignments

| Person | Role | Screen Time |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Person 1 | News anchor skit | ~10 sec on camera |
| Person 2 | Skit interruption + full demo narration | ~70 sec (mostly voiceover) |
| Person 3 | Technical explanation | ~20 sec on camera |
| Person 4 | Closing statement | ~20 sec on camera |

---

## Production Checklist

- [ ] Record skit scenes (1, 3, 4) with phone camera at eye level
- [ ] Screen record demo separately — pre-load everything, zero spinners
- [ ] Zoom browser to 110–125% for readability
- [ ] Record audio in a quiet room
- [ ] Edit in Clipchamp (free, Windows 11) or CapCut
- [ ] Add text overlays for key stats: "91.7%", "15.6x", "265 benchmarks"
- [ ] Export at 1080p
- [ ] Time it — if over 2:00, cut the recommender section first

---

## What to Cut If Over Time

In order of priority (cut from bottom up):

1. Recommender section (saves 10 sec)
2. Forecast section (saves 10 sec)
3. Paper mention — just flash it, no words (saves 5 sec)
4. Skit — replace with text-on-black-screen hook (saves 10 sec, last resort)

---

## Filming Tips

- The skit is 15 seconds. One take. Don't over-rehearse — slight awkwardness is the point.
- Person 1: dress shirt or blazer sells the anchor bit. Even over a t-shirt.
- The demo is the core of the video. Rehearse the mouse movements. Slow, deliberate.
- Person 4's close should be direct to camera, no notes. Memorize those two lines.
- Don't wait between scenes in editing — hard cuts feel more energetic in 2 minutes.
